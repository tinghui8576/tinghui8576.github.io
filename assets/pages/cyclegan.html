Mimicking Art Style using CycleGAN

Project Overview

The goal of the project is to generate realistic artwork from real-world photographs using Cycle-Consistent Adversarial Networks (CycleGAN). For this, we utilized the data from the Kaggle competition I’m Something of a Painter Myself , which includes Monet’s paintings and real-world images, and aimed to produce Monet-style art from this unpaired dataset. My contributions included implementing the CycleGAN model and trying to address issues like checkerboard artifacts and inconsistencies in details through the following methods:

  • Switching to MiFID/Hinge loss function: Enhanced the robustness and stability of the training process.
  • Introducing random noise: Mitigated issues related to vanishing or exploding gradients.
  • Adding fixed smooth convolutions: Ensured smoother transitions during forward and backward propagation.

Key Technologies Used
  • Software: Python, Cycle-Consistent Adversarial Networks (CycleGAN)
